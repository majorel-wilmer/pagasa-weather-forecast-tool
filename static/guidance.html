Why Davao and GenSan can differ: both currently use Metro Davao as the nearest available PAGASA five-day base forecast because General Santos is not a city in that specific PAGASA table. GenSan is physically in SOCCSKSARGEN, so it additionally receives SOCCSKSARGEN hazard statements from the weekly outlook. The base forecast and overlay are separate official products with different geographic scopes.

Base forecast

Daily condition, temperature and rain probability from PAGASA’s selected-city outlook. A proxy city is clearly named when the exact site is unavailable.

Regional overlay

Broader hazards such as enhanced Habagat, heavy rain, or cyclone-related gusts taken from PAGASA’s weekly outlook for the site’s actual region.

Probability vs intensity

Rain probability describes the chance of measurable rain. Light, moderate and heavy describe intensity. A 30% probability does not mean rain will be light if a storm develops.

Timing confidence

“Afternoon to evening” is qualitative. Exact start and end hours require short-range thunderstorm or rainfall advisories, radar and local observations.

Operational action guide

Green — light/general rain

  • Normal operations may continue with a rain contingency.
  • Move outdoor activities indoors when thunder is heard.
  • Check PAGASA radar before travel or field work.

Yellow — moderate

  • Monitor drainage and known flood-prone access roads.
  • Advise mobile teams to carry rain protection.
  • Review transport alternatives before peak rain periods.

Orange — heavy/at times heavy

  • Avoid non-essential travel through low-lying routes.
  • Prepare flexible staffing and remote-work contingencies.
  • Secure outdoor equipment and monitor local warnings frequently.

Red — admin override

  • Treat as an internal escalation requiring admin judgment.
  • Confirm against official warnings and local government instructions.
  • Document the reason and time of the escalation.

Official sources and limitations

  • Selected Philippine Cities Outlook
  • Weekly Weather Outlook
  • Daily Weather and Tropical Cyclone Information
  • PANaHON near-real-time observations and map
