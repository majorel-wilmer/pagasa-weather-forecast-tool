Live Map
Why Davao and GenSan now differ properly: every site is modeled from its own coordinates via Open-Meteo, so General Santos gets its own forecast instead of borrowing Metro Davao's numbers. There is no proxy-city substitution anywhere in this dashboard.

Base forecast

Daily condition, temperature and rain probability computed directly from Open-Meteo's hourly model output for each site's exact coordinates.

Model flag

Heavy-rain, thunderstorm or strong-gust flags are derived from the same hourly model data — not a separate regional bulletin.

Probability vs intensity

Rain probability describes the chance of measurable rain. Light, moderate and heavy describe modeled hourly intensity (mm/hr). A high probability does not by itself mean the rain will be heavy.

Timing confidence

The rain window (e.g. "2:00 PM – 5:00 PM") is calculated from hourly precipitation probability and amount, so it can shift as the model updates. It is model guidance, not an official advisory.

Operational action guide

Green — light/general rain

  • Normal operations may continue with a rain contingency.
  • Move outdoor activities indoors when thunder is heard.
  • Check the modeled rain window before travel or field work.

Yellow — moderate

  • Monitor drainage and known flood-prone access roads.
  • Advise mobile teams to carry rain protection.
  • Review transport alternatives before peak rain periods.

Orange — heavy/at times heavy

  • Avoid non-essential travel through low-lying routes.
  • Prepare flexible staffing and remote-work contingencies.
  • Secure outdoor equipment and monitor local warnings frequently.

Red — admin override

  • Treat as an internal escalation requiring admin judgment.
  • Confirm against official warnings and local government instructions.
  • Document the reason and time of the escalation.

Official sources and limitations

  • Open-Meteo — 5-day forecast, rain timing and intensity
  • Open-Meteo Forecast API documentation
  • PAGASA Daily Weather and Tropical Cyclone Information (storm advisories only)
  • PANaHON near-real-time observations and map

This dashboard is decision support, not an official warning system. Open-Meteo is a numerical model, not an official government forecast. Follow PAGASA, local DRRM offices, and evacuation instructions when warnings are issued.
